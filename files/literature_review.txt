Literature Review (References) - DeepFake Detection

Overview
DeepFake detection research emphasizes the arms race between increasingly realistic
synthetic media and detection systems. Recent surveys highlight that deep learning
dominates current approaches, but generalisation across unseen datasets and attack
types remains a key limitation. The literature also stresses the importance of
dataset diversity, temporal cues, and robust spatial feature extraction.

Key Findings from the Reviewed Papers
1) Surveys and Systematic Reviews
- Ahmed et al. provide a comprehensive review of visual DeepFake detection, grouping
  techniques by spatial, temporal, frequency, and spatio-temporal features and
  discussing generation tools and datasets. They emphasize that detection accuracy
  hinges on data quality and feature robustness across domains. [1]
- Ramanaharan et al. focus on model generalisation, showing that many detectors
  overfit to popular datasets (e.g., FaceForensics++, DFDC, Celeb-DF). Their study
  reports that generalisation is a persistent challenge and calls for broader,
  more diverse benchmarks and evaluation settings. [2]
- Kaur et al. review deepfake video detection challenges and opportunities, noting
  that deep learning methods dominate but often lack computational efficiency and
  generalisation. They highlight data imbalance, labelling limitations, and the
  need for real-time robust systems. [5]

2) Comparative Technique Reviews
- Khan et al. compare core deepfake detection architectures (e.g., CNN-LSTM,
  XceptionNet, MesoNet, capsule networks, and artifact-based methods). Their
  analysis shows strong performance from CNN-based detectors (XceptionNet, MesoNet)
  on FaceForensics++ while artifact-based methods offer competitive AUCs in
  certain datasets. They also summarize common datasets and emphasize the role
  of temporal modeling for video-level detection. [4]

3) Image-Focused DeepFake Detection
- Patel and Jain present a deepfake image detection study using machine learning
  and deep learning, describing preprocessing, dataset splitting, and CNN-based
  classification with transfer learning. Their findings reinforce that CNNs are
  effective for spatial artifact extraction in image-based detection tasks. [3]

Connection to This Project
The implementation in `code/deepfake_detection_with_high_accuracy.py` follows
the literature trends:
- Spatial feature extraction using a pretrained CNN backbone (EfficientNet) aligns
  with the strong performance of CNN-based detectors highlighted in [1,4,5].
- Temporal modeling via a transformer-based sequence model (Temporal ViT)
  addresses the need for temporal inconsistency detection, aligning with
  survey findings that temporal features improve video deepfake detection. [1,4]
- The emphasis on data preparation (face extraction, frame sampling, normalization)
  aligns with best practices described in [3] and the dataset quality concerns
  raised in [2,5].

Overall, the reviewed papers support the design choices in this project: combining
robust spatial feature extraction with temporal modeling, while recognizing that
generalisation across datasets remains a key research challenge.
